Aisha Rahman Profile

Patient Overview

Aisha Rahman is a 38-year-old customer support team lead who works rotating shifts and frequently changes between early and late work hours. Over the past year she has developed irregular sleep patterns, relies on convenience meals, and finds it difficult to keep a stable exercise routine.

Clinical Situation

At a recent primary care visit, Aisha was found to have elevated blood pressure and early cardiometabolic risk factors linked to poor sleep, inconsistent meals, weight gain, and chronic fatigue. Her doctor wants to intervene early with a structured 3-month program before these patterns develop into more serious hypertension and metabolic disease.

Why She Needs A 3-Month Program

Aisha needs a realistic plan that fits around shift work rather than a highly restrictive routine. The care team wants to use the next 3 months to stabilize her medication adherence, improve sleep consistency, reduce dietary risk, increase low-barrier movement, and monitor whether her blood pressure and energy patterns improve.

Care Goals

  • Improve blood pressure control with a repeatable morning monitoring and medication routine
  • Strengthen sleep habits through coaching and evening breathing work
  • Improve meal quality with simple breakfast, lunch, and dinner anchors
  • Build light activity into the week with low-friction walking sessions
  • Monitor progress through physician review, lab follow-up, and nutrition support
