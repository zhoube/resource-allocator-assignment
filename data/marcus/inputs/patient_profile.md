# Marcus Tan Profile

## Patient Overview
Marcus Tan is a 46-year-old operations manager with a busy desk-based job and long working hours. He often relies on takeout meals, has become less physically active over the last year, and reports poor sleep quality with frequent work-related stress.

## Clinical Situation
During a routine primary care visit, Marcus was found to have persistently elevated blood pressure and was diagnosed with hypertension. His doctor is concerned that his current lifestyle pattern may increase his long-term cardiovascular risk if left unmanaged.

## Why He Needs A 3-Month Program
Marcus is still functioning independently and working full-time, but he needs a structured program to build sustainable habits before his condition worsens. The care team wants to monitor whether a consistent routine of medication adherence, home monitoring, nutrition changes, exercise, and follow-up reviews can improve his blood pressure control over a 3-month period.

## Care Goals
- Improve blood pressure control through consistent medication adherence and home blood pressure checks
- Reduce sodium intake and improve meal quality through repeatable home routines
- Increase light-to-moderate physical activity with a schedule Marcus can sustain while working
- Lower stress load and improve sleep readiness through evening breathing and recovery work
- Review progress regularly with the physician, lab, dietitian, and health coach
