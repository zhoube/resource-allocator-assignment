# Grace Lim Profile

## Patient Overview
Grace Lim is a 59-year-old school administrator with type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and chronic knee pain that has gradually reduced her activity level. She works long office hours, often skips structured exercise, and finds it difficult to keep her meals and medication routine consistent during busy weeks.

## Clinical Situation
Her primary care physician is concerned about the combination of elevated blood pressure, glucose control risk, weight gain, and reduced mobility. Grace is still independent and working full-time, but her care team wants a structured 3-month program that addresses both cardiometabolic health and the physical limitations caused by knee discomfort.

## Why She Needs A 3-Month Program
Grace needs a plan that is practical enough to maintain while working, but structured enough to show measurable progress. The team wants to improve medication adherence, support glucose control, reduce cardiovascular risk, improve mobility, and build a repeatable routine of exercise, food planning, and follow-up care.

## Care Goals
- Improve blood pressure and glucose control through better medication adherence and home tracking
- Increase safe movement despite knee discomfort through physiotherapy-guided mobility work
- Use food planning and dietitian follow-up to improve meal quality and consistency
- Support weekly injectable medication adherence if prescribed
- Improve recovery and sleep consistency so the plan remains sustainable over the full 3 months
